SAFETY INCIDENT REPORT
Document ID: DOC-SAFE-001
Incident Date: 2026-01-28
Location: Production Line 3 — Assembly Area (line-assembly-1)
Reported by: Carlos Mendez, Shift Supervisor
Classification: Near-Miss (Severity Level 2)

1. INCIDENT DESCRIPTION
At approximately 14:35 on January 28, 2026, operator Lisa Zhang (person-lzhang) was performing a routine assembly operation on the Aerospace Component Kit (prod-aerospace-kit) workstation. During the pneumatic press operation, the safety interlock on Press Station #3 failed to engage, allowing the press cycle to initiate while the operator's hands were still in the work zone.

The operator withdrew her hands before the press completed its stroke. No injury occurred.

2. EQUIPMENT INVOLVED
- Press Station #3 on Assembly Line 1 (line-assembly-1)
- Pneumatic press model: AP-500 (associated with equip-press-ap500)
- Safety interlock: Dual palm button system + light curtain

3. ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS
- Primary cause: Light curtain sensor misalignment due to vibration from adjacent equipment.
- Contributing factor: Preventive maintenance on light curtain was overdue by 2 weeks.
- Contributing factor: Recent relocation of adjacent hydraulic unit increased vibration levels.

4. IMMEDIATE ACTIONS TAKEN
- Press Station #3 immediately locked out and tagged out.
- Light curtain realigned and tested — passed all functional checks.
- All operators on Line 1 notified of incident.
- Additional inspection of all light curtains on Assembly Lines 1 and 2.

5. CORRECTIVE ACTIONS
- CA-001: Add light curtain alignment to weekly PM checklist (Owner: Maria Chen, Due: 2026-02-05)
- CA-002: Install vibration dampening mounts on hydraulic unit (Owner: Maintenance, Due: 2026-02-15)
- CA-003: Review and update PM schedule for all safety-critical devices (Owner: David Park, Due: 2026-02-28)
- CA-004: Conduct safety stand-down meeting with all production staff (Owner: Carlos Mendez, Due: 2026-02-01)

6. LESSONS LEARNED
- Safety device maintenance must be prioritized over production schedules.
- Equipment relocation assessments must include impact analysis on adjacent safety systems.
- Near-miss reporting is critical — this incident prevented a potential crush injury.
