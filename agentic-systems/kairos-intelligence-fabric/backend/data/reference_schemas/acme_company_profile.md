# ACME Industrial Manufacturing — Company Profile

## Overview

ACME Industrial Manufacturing is a mid-size discrete manufacturing company headquartered in Dayton, Ohio. Founded in 1987, ACME specializes in precision-machined components, mechanical assemblies, and hydraulic systems for the aerospace, automotive, and industrial OEM sectors. The company operates two plants across Ohio with approximately 800 employees and generates annual revenue of ~$145M.

ACME's core competencies include CNC turning and milling of tight-tolerance parts (±0.005 mm), robotic welding of structural frames and brackets, hydraulic actuator assembly, and protective coating application. The company serves a diversified customer base ranging from Tier 1 aerospace suppliers to regional automotive parts distributors.

## Facilities

### Plant 1 — Dayton (Main Campus)
The Dayton facility is the original ACME campus, spanning 185,000 sq ft of manufacturing floor space. It houses the primary CNC machining operations across two cells: CNC Machining Cell A (line-cnc-01) and CNC Machining Cell B (line-cnc-02). The Dayton plant also operates the main Assembly Line 1 (line-assy-01) and the quality laboratory equipped with coordinate measuring machines (equip-cmm-01). Approximately 520 employees work at Plant 1, covering production, engineering, quality, and corporate functions. The facility runs two shifts Monday through Friday and a single shift on Saturdays during peak demand.

### Plant 2 — Columbus
The Columbus facility was opened in 2014 to expand ACME's welding and coating capabilities. The 95,000 sq ft plant houses Welding Station Alpha (line-weld-01), a secondary assembly area, and the coating line for zinc plating and powder coating operations. Plant 2 employs approximately 280 people and primarily supports high-volume bracket and frame production. Material handling between the two plants is managed via daily shuttle runs with a 24-hour transfer lead time.

## Manufacturing Processes

ACME operates a hybrid manufacturing model combining job-shop flexibility with repetitive line production:

**CNC Machining**: The backbone of ACME's operations. Six CNC machines handle turning, milling, and grinding operations. Key equipment includes two Okuma LB3000 EX lathes (equip-cnc-lathe-01, equip-cnc-lathe-02), a DMG MORI CMX 600V vertical mill (equip-cnc-mill-01), a Haas VF-4SS vertical mill (equip-cnc-mill-02), and a Studer S33 cylindrical grinder (equip-cnc-grind-01). Machining operations follow defined routings with typical sequences of rough turning → finish turning → milling → grinding → inspection.

**Robotic Welding**: Plant 2's Fanuc ARC Mate 120iD welding robot (equip-weld-rob-01) handles MIG and TIG welding for structural brackets and frames. Manual welding stations supplement robotic capacity for low-volume or complex geometries. All welders hold AWS D1.1 structural welding certifications.

**Assembly**: Assembly Line 1 (line-assy-01) supports both gear assembly and hydraulic actuator assembly operations. Workstations are configured in a U-cell layout for flexibility. Torque-controlled fastening, press-fit operations, and functional testing are performed inline.

**Coating & Surface Treatment**: Zinc plating, powder coating, and anodizing services are available at Plant 2. Coating specifications are driven by customer requirements and typically reference ASTM B633 (zinc) or MIL-PRF-8625 (anodize).

## Product Families

ACME's finished goods catalog includes approximately 85 active products organized into four families:

- **Precision Gear Assemblies**: Multi-stage gear trains for power transmission applications. Flagship product: Precision Gear Assembly (prod-gear-assy-100). Materials include AISI 4140 alloy steel and 8620 carburizing steel. Tolerances on gear teeth: AGMA Class 10-11.

- **Hydraulic Components**: Valve bodies, actuator blocks, and manifolds machined from aluminum 6061-T6 and ductile iron. Key product: Hydraulic Actuator Block (prod-hyd-act-200). These require leak-proof sealing surfaces with Ra ≤ 0.8 μm surface finish.

- **Custom Brackets & Frames**: Welded structural brackets and mounting systems for industrial and automotive applications. Key product: Custom Mounting Bracket (prod-brkt-mtg-300). Welded from A36 structural steel with zinc plating per customer spec.

- **Actuators**: Complete linear and rotary actuator assemblies integrating machined housings, seals, and electronic controls.

## Organizational Structure

ACME's leadership team reports to the CEO and is organized into the following departments:

- **Engineering** (~45 people): Product design, BOM management, process engineering, and tooling. Responsible for maintaining drawings, revision control, and first article inspection coordination.
- **Production** (~420 people): CNC operators, assemblers, welders, material handlers, and production supervisors across both plants. Managed by the VP of Operations.
- **Quality** (~35 people): Incoming inspection, in-process inspection, final inspection, SPC analysis, and ISO 9001 compliance. The Quality Manager reports directly to the Plant Manager.
- **Maintenance** (~30 people): Preventive, corrective, and predictive maintenance for all production equipment. Includes electricians, millwrights, and controls technicians.
- **Supply Chain** (~25 people): Procurement, inventory management, receiving, and shipping/logistics. Key suppliers include Precision Steel Solutions (supp-precision-steel), Allied Fasteners Co. (supp-allied-fasteners), and Midwest Bearings Inc. (supp-midwest-bearings).
- **Safety & EHS** (~10 people): Occupational safety, environmental compliance, and emergency response coordination.

## Key Challenges

ACME faces several persistent operational challenges:

1. **Tooling wear and tool life variability**: CNC tooling degrades at different rates depending on material hardness and cutting parameters. Unexpected tooling failures cause unplanned downtime and dimensional drift, particularly on the Okuma lathes (equip-cnc-lathe-01, equip-cnc-lathe-02) when machining 4140 steel.

2. **Tight tolerances and SPC compliance**: Aerospace customers require CPK ≥ 1.33 on critical dimensions. Maintaining statistical process control across multiple machines and operators is an ongoing challenge, especially after tool changes or machine warm-up.

3. **ISO 9001:2015 compliance**: ACME's QMS must satisfy annual surveillance audits. Nonconformance tracking, CAPA (Corrective and Preventive Action) closure rates, and document control are constant focus areas.

4. **On-time delivery pressure**: OEM customers impose strict delivery windows with penalties for late shipments. Balancing rush orders against planned production schedules creates frequent conflicts on the shop floor.

5. **Skilled labor shortage**: Experienced CNC machinists and certified welders are increasingly difficult to recruit. ACME runs an internal apprenticeship program but still faces a 12% annual turnover rate in skilled trades.

## Terminology and Abbreviations

- **OEE**: Overall Equipment Effectiveness = Availability × Performance × Quality
- **CPK**: Process Capability Index measuring how centered a process is within spec limits
- **SPC**: Statistical Process Control — control charts and capability studies
- **BOM**: Bill of Materials — structured list of components for a product
- **WO**: Work Order — production order for a specific quantity of a product
- **MWO**: Maintenance Work Order — scheduled or unscheduled maintenance task
- **FPY**: First Pass Yield — percentage of units passing inspection on first attempt
- **OTD**: On-Time Delivery — percentage of orders shipped by the required date
- **CAPA**: Corrective and Preventive Action — formal quality improvement process
- **PM**: Preventive Maintenance — scheduled maintenance based on time or usage intervals
- **MTBF**: Mean Time Between Failures — average operating hours between breakdowns
- **Ra**: Arithmetic average surface roughness in micrometers (μm)
- **AGMA**: American Gear Manufacturers Association — gear quality classification system
- **CMM**: Coordinate Measuring Machine — 3D precision measurement equipment
- **PPM**: Parts Per Million — defect rate metric used by automotive and aerospace customers
